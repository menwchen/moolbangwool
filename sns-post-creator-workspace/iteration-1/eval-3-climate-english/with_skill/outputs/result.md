## Research Summary

Climate change and ocean plastic pollution are deeply interconnected environmental crises. Key findings from established research:

- **Over 14 million tons** of plastic enter the ocean every year, making up roughly 80% of all marine debris (IUCN). At the current rate, plastic in the ocean is projected to outweigh fish by 2050 (Ellen MacArthur Foundation / World Economic Forum).
- **Plastic production is a climate problem**: The plastic lifecycle -- from extraction of fossil fuels, to manufacturing, to incineration -- accounts for approximately 3.4% of global greenhouse gas emissions (OECD, 2022). If plastic were a country, it would be the fifth-largest emitter of greenhouse gases.
- **Climate change worsens plastic pollution**: Rising sea levels, stronger storms, and increased flooding push more land-based plastic waste into the ocean. Warmer ocean temperatures also accelerate the breakdown of plastics into microplastics.
- **Microplastics are everywhere**: Microplastics have been found in the deepest ocean trenches (Mariana Trench), in Arctic sea ice, in human blood, and even in placentas. An estimated 5.25 trillion plastic particles currently float in the world's oceans.
- **Global Plastics Treaty**: The UN has been negotiating a legally binding international treaty to end plastic pollution (INC process), with negotiations ongoing through 2024-2025, aiming to address the full lifecycle of plastics.

Sources: IUCN, UNEP, OECD Global Plastics Outlook (2022), Ellen MacArthur Foundation, World Economic Forum, National Geographic
